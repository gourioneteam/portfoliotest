About Einstein

Albert Einstein was a German-born theoretical physicist who developed the theory of relativity, one of the two pillars of modern physics. His work is also known for its influence on the philosophy of science.

"Imagination is more important than knowledge. Knowledge is limited. Imagination encircles the world."

Early Life

Childhood and Education

Born on March 14, 1879, in Ulm, Germany, Einstein showed a brilliant curiosity about nature and science from an early age. Despite early speech difficulties, he went on to become one of history's most influential scientists.

Career Timeline

Major Achievements

Nobel Prize in Physics

Awarded in 1921 for his services to Theoretical Physics and discovery of the photoelectric effect.

Theory of Relativity

Developed the special and general theories of relativity, revolutionizing our understanding of space, time, and gravity.

Mass-Energy Equivalence

Formulated the world-famous equation E = mc², showing the relationship between mass and energy.

Legacy

Einstein's scientific breakthroughs fundamentally changed our understanding of the universe. His theories and discoveries have led to numerous practical applications, from nuclear power to GPS systems. His name has become synonymous with genius.
